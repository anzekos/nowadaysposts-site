## The pretty side of patriotic

You can welcome the holiday without going full bunting. Start at the front door with a **heart-shaped
patriotic wreath** — a soft, handmade wooden piece that says *come on in* all summer, not just on the
4th. Over the fireplace, a **satin American-flag bow garland** drapes red, white and blue across the
mantel, shelf or stair rail in two minutes flat and packs away to reuse every year. For shelves and
trays, a **vintage 4th of July tiered-tray set** keeps the Americana farmhouse-soft instead of kitschy,
while a **framed wildflower American flag wall art** reimagines the stars and stripes in painterly
blooms — cottagecore meets Americana, pretty enough to leave up well past the cookout.

## Windows, done right

The fastest way to make a room feel finished is to fix the windows. **Thermal blackout curtains** — one
of Amazon's all-time bestselling panels — block light and soften street noise while reading like calm,
high-end drapery instead of a hotel shade. They keep the bedroom dark for sleeping in and cozy
year-round, in neutral colors that go with everything you already own.

## The everyday anchors: vases, frames and flattering light

Finish with the small pieces that read expensive. A **set of 3 neutral ceramic vases** (one with a
handle) groups into an instant modern-farmhouse centerpiece — lovely empty or holding a few dried stems
— while a set of **graduated glass cylinder vases** in 4, 6, 8 and 10-inch heights cluster for a layered
table moment or float a candle each. Working with less space? A **12-pack of mini glass bud vases**
turns single blooms into a whole tablescape down a counter or windowsill. On the wall, a **thin-border
18x24 poster frame** with polished plexiglass anchors a bare wall over the sofa or bed, and in the
bedroom, a **wall-mounted lighted vanity mirror** (cordless, rechargeable, 1X/10X magnification) turns
any corner into a flattering little glam station. Mark the Fourth with a few easy touches, then let
these anchors carry the room the rest of the year.
